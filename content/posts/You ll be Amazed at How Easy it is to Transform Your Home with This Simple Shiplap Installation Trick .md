### A. Preparing the Room

The first step is to prepare the room for installation. This includes removing any existing trim or molding and filling any holes or cracks in the wall. You should also make sure that the wall is clean and free of dust and debris. 

### B. Measuring and Cutting the Shiplap

Once the room is prepped, it’s time to measure and cut the shiplap. Measure the length and width of the wall and mark the measurements on the shiplap boards. Cut the boards to the desired length and width with a saw. 

### C. Installing the Shiplap

Once the boards are cut, it’s time to install them. Start at the top of the wall and work your way down. Use a level to make sure the boards are even and use nails to secure them to the wall.
