# You Won't Believe How Easy it is to Make this Classic Paper Airplane!

Making a paper airplane is a classic activity that has been enjoyed by children and adults alike for generations. It's a fun and easy way to explore the principles of aerodynamics and have a great time in the process. Read on to learn how to make a classic paper airplane and get some tips for flying it.

## Overview of Paper Airplanes

Paper airplanes are a simple and inexpensive way to explore the principles of aerodynamics. All you need is a sheet of paper and some basic folding skills. With just a few folds, you can turn a single sheet of paper into a flying machine.

## Benefits of Making a Paper Airplane

Making a paper airplane is a great way to spend time with your family or friends. It's also a great way to learn about the principles of aerodynamics and have fun at the same time. Plus, it's a great way to stay creative and explore your own paper airplane designs.

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Gather the Materials

To make a paper airplane, you'll need a single sheet of paper and a pair of scissors. You may also want to have a ruler or other straight edge handy to help you make straight folds.

### Fold the Paper

Start by folding the paper in half lengthwise. Then, unfold the paper and fold each side in to meet the center crease. Finally, fold the paper in half again.

### Make the Wings

Next, fold the wings of the paper airplane. Start by folding the top corners of the paper down to meet the bottom edge. Then, fold the wings up along the creases you just made.

### Attach the Wings

Finally, you'll need to attach the wings to the body of the paper airplane. To do this, fold the wings back along the creases you made earlier. Then, use a bit of tape or glue to secure the wings in place.
